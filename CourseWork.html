Faling to account for business processes'

This relates to the tools that a development team might use, this might be a reason becasue the tool that they think might solve a problem in there development process doesnt adhere to normal protocol in the business' workflow, which then burdens the whole development team into maintaing an insufferable tool that didn't help.

Investing in technology for the company and not customer value

This normally happens if a company invests in new tech/equipment, lets say a CEO said you should buy, but neglected to fully understand whether it is necessary to invest in such equimpment in a business stand point or whether if it's a neccessity to the customer. also to take into account whether you get any return of investment (ROI).

Poor choice of vendor

This is probably the ,ost common of reasons why digital projects tend to fall through, this tends to happen because if your a small client compared to what they usually deal with, they may deprioritize your work, but on the other hand you may be a massive client for them and might not be able to accommodate what your asking for effectively therefore there being a breakdown between the vendor and the client.

Overambition

The idea of expecting perfection is a downfall for any digital project, and majority of the time people with this frame of mind tend to fail neglecting to consider whether they have took on more than they can chew.
